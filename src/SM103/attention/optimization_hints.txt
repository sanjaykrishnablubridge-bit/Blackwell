What the profile actually says
1. You are barrier-bound, not memory- or math-bound. Warp State shows ~7.6 of 13.3 cycles/instruction in Stall Barrier (Nsight's estimated local speedup: 57%). Eligible warps per scheduler is 0.34, issued 0.30, and 69.9% of cycles have no eligible warp. cuDNN's dominant stall is Long Scoreboard (normal for a pipelined memory kernel); yours is bar.sync. All 512 threads are repeatedly meeting at CTA-wide __syncthreads().
2. You're moving the attention matrix through shared memory; cuDNN isn't. Shared loads: 1.05B instructions (+2,100,000% vs cuDNN — cuDNN has essentially none), shared stores 644M. LSU is your hottest pipe (31.65%, +3,788% vs cuDNN) while your Tensor pipe sits at 4.76% vs cuDNN's ~50%, and TMEM utilization ~6% vs ~52%. You are using tcgen05 MMA (utchmma bf16→fp32 shows 2.1e12 ops) but achieving ~10% of cuDNN's MMA ops/cycle. The tensor cores are starved by smem round-trips and barriers.
3. The smem traffic you do have is conflicted. 27.6% of shared-load wavefronts hit bank conflicts (40.1M conflicts, est. 12.5% speedup), 10.3% of stores (est. 4.6%). Uncoalesced shared accesses: 19% excessive wavefronts (est. 18%), pinpointed at lines 5369–5370. Notably, Shared Load Matrix = 0 — you're not using ldmatrix/stmatrix at all.
4. Instruction bloat. 6.2B executed instructions, +438% vs cuDNN for the same problem. Integer (address math) and Load/Store dominate. 104M non-fused FP32 FADDs at lines 5483–5487 (est. 4.6% from FFMA fusion). Avg divergent branches +62,689% — the causal mask is being handled with per-element branches. Warp-stall sampling puts 51% of all samples on line 5411 (almost certainly your barrier/wait).
5. HBM is irrelevant here. DRAM throughput 0.42%, L2 hit rate 91%+. Also: 786K plain global loads and 405K global stores exist that cuDNN doesn't have (cuDNN is 100% TMA), and occupancy is capped at 1 CTA/SM by the 232 KB smem (25% theoretical = achieved, so occupancy per se isn't the killer — the dead time within the CTA is).
Experiment plan (in order of expected payoff)
None of these change numerics meaningfully: keep bf16 inputs, fp32 accumulation, fp32 softmax with max-subtraction throughout.
Experiment 1 — Warp specialization + mbarrier pipeline (targets the 57% barrier stall).
Split the 512 threads into roles: 1 producer warp(group) issuing TMA loads for K/V tiles, 1 warp driving tcgen05.mma (on Blackwell a single thread can launch the MMA), and consumer warpgroups doing softmax/correction. Replace every CTA-wide __syncthreads() with mbarrier arrive/wait pairs (producer→consumer full/empty semantics) and named barriers scoped to a warpgroup where intra-group sync is needed. Build a 2–4 stage circular buffer for KV tiles so TMA of tile i+2 overlaps MMA of tile i+1 and softmax of tile i. This is the single biggest lever — it's what turns the green cuDNN profile shape (long-scoreboard-dominated) from your blue one (barrier-dominated). Verify with: Stall Barrier cycles/inst, Issued warp/scheduler, Tensor pipe %.
Experiment 2 — Eliminate the S/P smem round-trip (targets the 1.05B LDS / 31% LSU pipe).
The score matrix S = QKᵀ lands in TMEM on sm_10x. Read it with tcgen05.ld straight into registers, do the row-max/exp/row-sum entirely in registers (warp-shuffle reductions for the row statistics rather than smem reductions), and feed P back to the second MMA without staging it through shared memory (tcgen05.st to TMEM, or if your P·V path consumes from smem, write with stmatrix in the exact swizzled layout the MMA descriptor expects). Every STS/LDS of S/P you remove kills LSU work and a barrier. This plus Experiment 1 is essentially the FA3/FA4-style Blackwell design cuDNN implements. Verify with: Shared Load/Store instruction counts (should drop by orders of magnitude), LSU pipe %.
Experiment 3 — Swizzle the remaining shared memory (targets 40M bank conflicts, lines 5369–5370).
For the KV staging buffers, use the TMA 128B swizzle mode and matching MMA smem descriptors so tiles are loaded and consumed conflict-free; for anything hand-indexed, apply XOR-swizzled layouts (or ldmatrix, currently unused) instead of padding. Cheap to do, ~12–18% by Nsight's own estimate. Verify: L1 Wavefronts Shared Excessive at those two lines → ~0.
Experiment 4 — Fuse the softmax arithmetic (lines 5483–5487).
Rewrite the rescale/accumulate as FFMA: fold the 1/ln2 scale into the QK scale so exp becomes exp2f(fma(s, scale_log2e, -m*scale_log2e)), and the running-output correction as o = fma(o, alpha, p*v) style updates. Use exp2f → MUFU.EX2 on the XU pipe (note cuDNN's XU utilization is ~46% vs your ~6% — theirs is spending its "FP32" budget on EX2, yours on unfused FADDs). Max-subtraction stays, so numerical stability is unchanged; only ULP-level rounding order differs, precision class (fp32) is identical. Est. ~5% alone, more once instruction count matters post-Exp-1/2.
Experiment 5 — Predicated causal masking + tile-level skipping.
Replace per-element if masking with branchless select (s = mask ? s : -inf compiled to predication, or add a precomputed −inf bias tile), skip entirely-masked KV tiles before issuing their TMA/MMA at all, and only run the masking code on the single diagonal tile per row-block. This removes the divergent branches and a chunk of the integer/control instruction bloat. Also rebalance the grid: with causal, diagonal blocks do variable work — schedule row-blocks so long and short rows mix per SM (or see Exp 7).
Experiment 6 — Kill the residual non-TMA global traffic.
You still issue 786K global loads and 405K global stores. Move the O writeback to cp.async.bulk.tensor (TMA store, ideally from smem staging written with stmatrix), and route whatever the global loads are (seq-length metadata, head pointers) through constant/uniform loads or fold them into TMA descriptors. cuDNN's profile has zero of these.
Experiment 7 — Launch-shape experiments (cheap A/B tests).
(a) 1536 CTAs / 148 SMs = 10.4 waves — try a persistent kernel with an atomic tile counter to remove wave quantization and improve causal load balance (Nsight shows ~5.4% imbalance). (b) Try cluster size 2 with tcgen05 MMA cta_group::2 so a CTA pair shares one larger MMA and K/V smem via DSMEM — halves per-CTA smem loads and doubles effective tile size. (c) After Exp 2 shrinks smem needs, test ≤116 KB/block so 2 CTAs/SM fit (32 warps/SM) vs today's 1 — with a proper pipeline this may not matter, but it's a one-line test. (d) You're at 64 regs/thread with 25% occupancy headroom; cuDNN uses 128. Raise __launch_bounds__/maxrregcount to 128 and keep the softmax state in registers — you're currently paying smem traffic to compensate for registers you aren't using.
Experiment 8 — Instruction diet.
After the structural fixes, attack the +438% instruction count: hoist loop-invariant address math out of the KV loop, use the uniform datapath (UR registers) for per-CTA-constant addressing, precompute TMA/MMA descriptors once, and unroll the inner loop with compile-time offsets. Check Executed Instructions converges toward cuDNN's.
How I'd sequence it
Do 3 and 4 first (days, low risk, ~15–20% combined) to build measurement discipline, then 1+2 together since they're one architectural change (the warp-specialized TMEM-resident pipeline) — that's where the bulk of the 9.4× gap closes, because it converts barrier-idle cycles into tensor-pipe cycles. Then 5, 6, 7, 8 as tuning passes. After each step, re-profile and watch four numbers: Stall Barrier (→ under ~1 cycle/inst), Tensor pipe % (→ 40–50%), Shared Load instructions (→ near zero), Issued warp/scheduler (→ 0.6+). For precision, keep a fixed test harness comparing against an fp64 reference with max-abs and RMS error per head — expressed that way, every change above should sit inside the same error envelope you have today, since accumulation precision, softmax stability trick, and datatypes are all untouched.
One caveat: exact line-level fixes (5369–5370, 5411, 5483–5487) assume the source correlation in the report maps to your v34 source; if you can share those regions of the kernel, I can suggest the specific rewrites rather than the patterns.
