0.686 ms / 310 TFLOPS, correctness green on both O and LSE — that landed almost exactly in the middle of the predicted 0.6–0.8 window (2.33× over v42). Now the profile has fundamentally changed character: the sync-and-shuffle waste is gone, and what remains is the structure of the schedule. Here's the path to ~0.2 ms, ranked by expected payoff.
1. The big one: double-buffer S in TMEM and pipeline tiles (~1.5–2× on its own). Your per-tile chain is still strictly serial: QKᵀ → wait → softmax on CUDA cores → P store → PV → wait. The tensor cores sit idle during every softmax, and softmax is your longest CUDA-core stretch (128-wide max reduction + 128 EX2 per row). The fix is to issue QKᵀ(n+1) into a second S buffer the moment S(n) has been read into registers, so the tensor cores compute the next score tile underneath the current softmax. One layout detail matters: S needs 128 fp32 columns, so two S buffers + O don't fit in your current 256-column allocation. But TMEM has 512 columns per SM — bump the tcgen05.alloc to 512 and lay out S0@[0,128), S1@[128,256), O@[256,320), P@[320,384), M/CORR@384/385. Putting P in its own region (instead of overwriting S's columns) is also what breaks the false dependency that currently forces PV(n) to finish before QKᵀ(n+1) can start. This is exactly what cuDNN's 48-UTCHMMA/16-UTCBAR structure is doing.
2. Gate the O rescale on corr != 1.0f (cheap, measurable). After the first few tiles the running max rarely changes, so corr == 1.0 and the LDTM→FMUL→STTM round-trip is a no-op you're paying for anyway. One care point: tcgen05.ld/st are warp-collective, so gate with if(!__all_sync(0xffffffff, corr == 1.0f)) { ...round-trip... } rather than per-thread predication.
3. Re-examine the K load path. Your make_tma_3d_katom moves K as 16-byte atoms with a stride — that's the least efficient shape TMA can be asked to do (many small strided transactions). Now that you have the SW128 machinery working for V, the same trick applies to K: load K with a plain 2D SWIZZLE_128B map (full 128 B rows, D=64) and use the K-major SW128 descriptor for the QKᵀ B operand. Same zero-shuffle property, but the TMA traffic becomes dense 128 B lines. Whether this matters depends on whether you're TMA-bound at all — which brings me to:
4. Profile before choosing between 1 and 3. Rerun the same PC-sampling capture on v43nr. Where the samples pile up now is the decision: mostly on the mma_bar wait after QKᵀ → you're MMA/softmax-serialization bound → do #1 first. Mostly on lbarK/lbarV waits in the compute group → you're load-bound → do #3 first (and consider 3-deep buffering of K/V instead of 2). My money is on the former given only 85 GB/s of bandwidth used, but one capture settles it.
5. Smaller items after those: issue the diagonal tile's QKᵀ as a shorter MMA chain instead of computing then masking (saves a few percent on causal); and once pipelining exists, consider splitting the MMA-issue role out of tid==0 of the softmax group into its own warp so descriptor bookkeeping never steals cycles from the exp loop (this is the register-pressure split your own v43 header comment in the v2 file was already reaching toward).
Realistic trajectory: #1 should land you around 0.35–0.45 ms; #2/#3/#5 together push toward ~0.25–0.3; the last stretch to cuDNN's 0.21 is grind (their softmax is fully hidden and their waits are the only samples left).
